# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, developers, and CEOs who need to understand Khaled Adel's range quickly: full-stack product development, AI/ML capability, biomedical/scientific background, and creative technical execution. They are likely scanning the site to judge whether he can build real products, collaborate with technical teams, communicate with decision-makers, and bring a distinctive creative edge to engineering work.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio presents Khaled as a creative full-stack developer with a rare overlap of deployed product engineering, AI/ML, biomedical science, and scientific visualization. Success means visitors can understand his positioning in the first viewport, inspect credible shipped projects, see evidence of technical range and creative judgment, and find a direct contact path without friction.

## Brand Personality

Bold, vibrant, smart. The tone should feel technically competent, visually memorable, and practical without becoming cold, corporate, or generic. The portfolio should communicate useful range: not a scattered list of skills, but a coherent practice that connects full-stack delivery, AI/ML, scientific thinking, and visual craft.

## Anti-references

Avoid corporate resume style, the generic dark-purple developer portfolio pattern, gradient text as decoration, identical icon-card grids, vague "passionate developer" copy, and layouts that bury shipped work behind ornamental effects. Avoid treating scientific illustration as a novelty section; it should feel like part of the same technical practice.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the positioning: creative full-stack developer should be visible immediately.
2. Show proof before claims: shipped projects, live links, technologies, and illustration work carry more weight than self-description.
3. Make range legible: group work into clear themes so breadth reads as strategy, not noise.
4. Keep motion purposeful: animation should guide attention and reinforce technical craft without making the page hard to scan.
5. Design for scanning: visitors should be able to understand the page in minutes, then dive into detail where needed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible links and controls, visible focus states, semantic sections, useful alt text for images, and reduced-motion behavior for users who prefer less animation. Motion can be polished and expressive, but it should not block reading or navigation.
